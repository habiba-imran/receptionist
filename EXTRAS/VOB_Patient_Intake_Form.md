## **CALL SCRIPT & TALKING POINTS** 

_Keep it warm and brief. The goal is clean card data — not a sales call._ 

## **Opening** 

“Hi, may I speak with [patient name]? This is [your name] calling on behalf of [practice name]. I'm helping verify your insurance benefits so your visit is processed correctly and there are no billing surprises. Do you have a couple of minutes, and could you grab your insurance card?” 

## **Capturing the card** 

“Perfect. Could you read me the front of the card exactly as it's printed — the insurance company name, the member ID, and the group number? Then flip it over and read the payer ID and the customer-service number on the back.” 

- Read the member ID back to them digit by digit to confirm — transposed IDs are the #1 cause of failed verifications. 

- If a field is blank on the card, write “not listed” rather than guessing. 

## **Subscriber check** 

“Is the insurance under your own name, or is it through a spouse, parent, or employer? If it's not yours, I'll just need that person's name, date of birth, and your relationship to them.” 

## **Secondary coverage / COB** 

“Do you have any other insurance — a second plan, a spouse's plan, or Medicare alongside this one? If so, which one do you consider your primary?” 

- If they have two plans, capture both and note which is primary — this prevents COB denials, which are common in nephrology and behavioral health. 

## **Service context** 

“Last couple of questions — do you know the reason for your upcoming visit, and were you referred by another doctor? Has anyone already set up an authorization for this, or have you seen another provider for the same issue recently?” 

## **Close** 

“That's everything I need — thank you. We'll verify your benefits with the insurance company and reach out if anything else is needed. Have a great day.” 

**Reminder:** The patient confirms identity and card data only. Deductible, coverage limits, copay, and authorization requirements are verified directly with the payer after this call.
